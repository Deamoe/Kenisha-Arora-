About Ms. Kenisha Arora

Kenisha Arora is a dynamic youth leader and undergraduate student studying Medical Sciences in Canada. Her remarkable contributions to the community and the global arena have led her to become featured on Forbes Magazine and receive the honor of being a Top 10 Winner of the esteemed $100,000 Chegg Global Student Prize.

Representation and Leadership at the United Nations

As an inspiring leader, Ms. Arora firmly believes in the power of education as a catalyst for change. She has demonstrated this conviction through her work with UNESCO’s Youth Network, where she serves as UNESCO’s official Youth Representative for Europe and North America, leading a group of over 100 young education advocates striving to transform education across the globe. Kenisha has also been elected as the global Youth & Student Representative on the United Nations’ SDG4 High-Level Steering Committee through which she collaborates with high-ranking officials such as the Director General of UNESCO, Executive Director of UNICEF, Managing Partner of the World Bank, and various Heads of State to oversee global education governance.

As the youngest member of the UN’s High-Level Committee, Kenisha has set new milestones for the UN in youth engagement. Her pioneering accomplishments in this role include:

  • Discussing education financing to end learning poverty with Ministers of Finance and Education at the historical gathering of the World Bank Spring Meetings.

  • Speaking at High-Level Opening of the United Nations Assembly Summit on Education Marked a significant moment for youth representation on the international stage

  • Publishing an Open Letter to Member States with the President of the UN Assembly Urging world leaders to transform education and ensure youth engagement. This collaboration signified the recognition of youth as equal partners in driving change.

  • Launching a Youth Declaration with UN Secretary-General Antonio Guterres that underscores the critical role young people play in transforming societies through education. Kenisha brought together the perspectives of 500,000 youth from 170+ countries

  • Successfully passed the first ever youth engagement indicator in the UN SDG framework , ensuring that youth voices are not only heard but actively involved in decision-making and action towards sustainable development. This milestone achievement has established youth involvement as a cornerstone of the global development agenda

National Advocacy

Kenisha's advocacy efforts extend beyond the global stage; she also served as a School Board Trustee, representing over 150,000 students. During her tenure, she worked with the Ministry of Education to address systemic inequities and passed a motion to provide free menstrual hygiene products in all school washrooms.

Education Policy and Corporate Governance

At her University, Kenisha worked with the University Deans to develop a strategic plan that reimagines the future of higher education and focused on re-energizing research and scientific advancements, fostering a sense of community, and promoting teaching and learning for the social impact. As a Senator at her University and Chair of the Student Senate, she continues to influence academic policy and represent the voices of 40,000 students. Additionally, Kenisha serves as a Governor on the University's Board of Governors and oversees a budget of $829 million to fulfill fiduciary responsibilities and ensure a bright future for all students.

Restoring Humanity through Global Philanthropy

During the COVID19 pandemic, Kenisha was determined to restore humanity back in her community. After learning about the struggles of vulnerable members, Kenisha and her sister founded The HopeSisters, a non-profit dedicated towards spreading hope.

  • What started as a small initiative to restore hope during the COVID19 pandemic turned into movement of hope with over 5000+ HopeSpreaders and chapters across the world

  • The HopeSisters launched Project HopeBags to uplift children in foster care and orphanages and have already supported thousands of children.

  • Their chapters in Europe support Ukrainian children who have fled from the war and provide them with essential items and education opportunities through HopeBags

  • In India and Liberia, The HopeSisters are supporting girls in rural villages with getting access to basic foundational learning

The HopeSisters’ philanthropic efforts have led them to receive the Princess Diana Award, the $20,000 Founders Award from Ivey School of Business, and gain support from the Gates Foundation, United Nations Canada, and many government officials.
